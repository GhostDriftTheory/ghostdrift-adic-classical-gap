Preface: The Context of Classical Education in Japan

To grasp the full scope of this paper, international readers must understand the unique structure of the Japanese high school curriculum. The subject of "Japanese" (Kokugo) mandates the study of three distinct fields:

  1. Modern Japanese (Gendai-bun): Contemporary literature and logical writing.
  2. Classical Japanese (Kobun): Literature from the Heian period (approx. 794–1185) onwards.
  3. Classical Chinese (Kanbun): Ancient Chinese texts (often pre-modern) read via a specialized Japanese annotation system (Kundoku) that rearranges the syntax for Japanese speakers.

The "Classics Obsolescence Argument" (Koten Fuyō-ron) is a recurring cultural debate in Japan. Critics argue that the compulsory study of Kobun and Kanbun imposes an excessive time cost on students with negligible practical utility in modern society. This paper attempts to formalize this domestic debate into a verifiable mathematical structure using the ADIC protocol.

Abstract This paper proposes a methodology to decouple the "Classics Obsolescence" argument—a prevalent discourse in Japanese education asserting that Classical Japanese and Classical Chinese are unnecessary—from emotional rhetoric, separating it into verifiable finite facts (Fact) and subjective values (Value). By reducing the primary claims of the obsolescence argument to factual propositions concerning "Time Cost," "International Precedents," and "Learning Efficacy," we design an ADIC (Anti-Doxa Immutable Certificate) protocol to verify their consistency with existing datasets (Ledgers). Adopting a $\Sigma_1$-type (existential) refutation logic, this protocol aims to mathematically invalidate unfounded societal discourse through the rigorous presentation of finite counter-examples.

1. Introduction: Rendering Invisible Dynamics Observable

1.1 Implicit Dynamics in Educational Discourse

In contemporary educational discourse, the assertion that "classics are useless" often circulates as if it were a self-evident truth, despite a lack of empirical evidence. This phenomenon represents a form of invisible societal pressure that influences decision-making without formal verification. To counter this unsubstantiated discourse, emotional rebuttal is insufficient; what is required is a formal barrier (ADIC) that mathematically decomposes the argument's components and demonstrates their inconsistency with observable facts.

1.2 Purpose of this Study

The objective of this study is not to prove the absolute value of classical education. Rather, it is to demonstrate, in a computable format, that the "factual assumptions" implicitly premised by the obsolescence argument contradict objective data. This provides a mechanism to filter the debate into "invalid claims based on factual errors" and "pure value judgments."

2. Formulation of the Argument and Claim Types

From the logs of obsolescence discourse, we extract and define four representative Claim Types. This paper primarily targets C1 and C4, which are readily amenable to objective verification, and treats C3 as auxiliary.

Claim Type C1: Excessive Time Cost
  • Claim: "High school students are forced to spend an enormous amount of time on classics, squeezing out other subjects."
  • Fact Candidate: A claim that implies $\text{RequiredHours}_{\text{Classics}} \ge H_0$ for some integer threshold $H_0 > 0$.
    (Note: Although $H_0=100$ is used for convenience in this paper, refutation is possible for any $H_0 > 70$ given the existence of actual data (70 hours).)
Claim Type C2: Lack of Utility
  • Claim: "Classics are completely useless in real life."
  • Fact Candidate (C2a): The statistical proposition that "classical education has no positive correlation or utility in any occupation or career path."
  • Value (C2b): The value judgment that "education should only pursue modern practicality."
    (Note: In this paper, C2 is explicitly excluded from ADIC verification. This is due to principle impossibility. The concept of "useful/useless" cannot be defined on a finite integer table (Ledger) as an agreeable observable fact, and thus cannot be reduced to a $\Sigma_1$-type existential proof. Therefore, C2 is structurally treated as PureValue rather than Fact.)
Claim Type C3: Zero Effect
  • Claim: "Studying classics does not contribute to the improvement of reading comprehension or logical thinking skills (Zero Effect)."
  • Fact Candidate: A claim that assumes, for a fixed rational threshold $\delta_{\mathrm{zero}} > 0$: $$ \forall \text{Study},\ |\text{EffectSize}(\text{Study})| \le \delta_{\mathrm{zero}} $$
Claim Type C4: International Obsolescence
  • Claim: "Classical education has already been abolished in advanced foreign countries."
  • Fact Candidate: A claim that assumes, for a certain non-negative integer threshold $H_{\mathrm{zero}} \ge 0$: $$ \forall \text{Country} \in \text{G7},\ \text{Hours}_{\text{Classics}}(\text{Country}) \le H_{\mathrm{zero}} $$ (Note: This paper defines "advanced foreign countries" as the G7 nations for practical frequency. Trends in non-G7 countries can be handled by extending the Ledger with a separate Claim, e.g., C4'.)

2.1 Ledger Snapshot Condition (Immutable Snapshot)

Each Ledger $L_{\mathrm{curr}}, L_{\mathrm{intl}}, L_{\mathrm{study}}$ used in this paper is fixed as a snapshot as of December 9, 2025.

Raw data (such as jp_curriculum_time_cost) is type-converted as necessary (e.g., mapping "present" to 9999, extracting effect sizes), and the resulting finite sequences are frozen as $L_{\mathrm{curr}}^*, L_{\mathrm{intl}}^*, L_{\mathrm{study}}^*$. The ADIC certificate includes the hash values of these snapshots: $$ (\mathrm{Hash}(L_{\mathrm{curr}}^*),\ \mathrm{Hash}(L_{\mathrm{intl}}^*),\ \mathrm{Hash}(L_{\mathrm{study}}^*)) $$ By publishing this set, the "set of facts at that point in time" relied upon by the certificate can be uniquely reproduced regardless of future Ledger updates, guaranteeing third-party verifiability of the $\Sigma_1$-type refutation.

3. Data Layer: ADIC Ledger Specifications

In this paper, actual datasets are adopted directly as ADIC Ledgers. Each Ledger is a finite matrix where every row is a fully determined record. Note that the purpose of this paper is to present the logical structure of ADIC, not to conduct an exhaustive meta-analysis. Additional data such as PISA data or long-term tracking studies would be processed simply as additional Ledger rows within the ADIC framework, without affecting the structure itself.

4. Logical Structure and $\Sigma_1$-Type Verification

The core of ADIC is to refute the universal fact claims contained in the obsolescence argument using $\Sigma_1$-type (existential) propositions. Since the negation of a universal proposition is an existential proposition, it is established by presenting a single counter-example (Witness), making the verification cost low and logically robust.

4.1–4.3 Formulation of Refutation Propositions

Definition 4.1 (Refutation Propositions).
For each obsolescence argument $P$, the refutation proposition $R$ is defined as follows:
  • C1 (Time Cost): $$ P_{\mathrm{C1}}(H_0) \implies R_{\mathrm{C1}}(H_0) := \exists r \in L_{\mathrm{curr}} \text{ s.t. } (\text{IsRequired}(r) = 1 \land r.\text{TotalHours} < H_0) $$
  • C4 (International): $$ P_{\mathrm{C4}}(H_{\mathrm{zero}}) \implies R_{\mathrm{C4}}(H_{\mathrm{zero}}) := \exists r \in L_{\mathrm{intl}} \text{ s.t. } r.\text{TotalHours} > H_{\mathrm{zero}} $$
  • C3 (No Effect): $$ P_{\mathrm{C3}} \implies R_{\mathrm{C3}} := \exists r \in L_{\mathrm{study}} \text{ s.t. } (\text{EffectSign}(r) \neq 0) $$

4.4 Definition of $\Sigma_1$-Type Verify Functions

We define primitive recursive functions (pseudo-code) to check each refutation proposition.

4.4.1 Verify Function for C1

function Verify_C1(H0, L_curr):
    for i from 0 to len(L_curr) - 1:
        r := L_curr[i]
        # required_or_elective="Required" corresponds to IsRequired(r)=1 by definition
        if (r.required_or_elective == "Required") and (r.total_hours < H0): return r
    return ⊥

4.4.2 Verify Function for C4

function Verify_C4(H_zero, L_intl):
    for i from 0 to len(L_intl) - 1:
        r := L_intl[i]
        if r.total_hours > H_zero: return r
    return ⊥

4.4.3 Verify Function for C3

function Verify_C3(L_study):
    for i from 0 to len(L_study) - 1:
        r := L_study[i]
        # effect_direction ≠ "0" corresponds to EffectSign(r) ≠ 0 by definition
        if r.effect_direction != "0": return r
    return ⊥

(Note: This function halts as soon as it discovers one row satisfying the refutation condition.)

5. Decision Process for ADIC Status

The following algorithm assigns a status to any obsolescence claim $C$.

  1. Decomposition: $C \to (\text{Fact } F, \text{Value } V)$
  2. Matching: Determine if $F$ falls under $\{P_{\mathrm{C1}}, P_{\mathrm{C4}}, P_{\mathrm{C3}}\}$.
  3. Witness Search: If matched, search for a witness $w$ from Ledger $L^\star$.
  4. Judgment:
    • Refuted_by_Data: Witness $w$ found ($\Sigma_1$ proof).
    • Unsupported: $F$ exists but no witness found (undetermined).
    • PureValue: No meaningful $F$ (subjective only).

5.1 ADIC Certificate Object

An ADIC Certificate is defined as a finite-length record (e.g., JSON) with the following fields: 

{
  "claim_type": "C1_TimeCost",
  "status": "Refuted_by_Data",
  "ledger_hashes": [ "Hash(L_curr*)", ... ],
  "verify_fn": "Verify_C1",
  "verify_params": { "H0": 100 },
  "witness_pointer": { "ledger": "L_curr", "row_id": "JP_HS_..." }
}

6. $\Sigma_1$ Certificates at the Arithmetic Level

In this section, we rigorously define constants and encoding to complete the ADIC system within the language of Peano Arithmetic (PA).

6.1 Fixing Constants and Sensitivity Analysis

We fix the following constants at the arithmetic level: $$ H_0 := 100,\qquad H_{\mathrm{zero}} := 0,\qquad \delta_{\mathrm{zero}} := \frac{1}{3}. $$ These values are examples of formalizing the obsolescence argument, and the protocol is parametric with respect to these constants. Given the existence of real data (e.g., Gengo Bunka 70 hours, Hauspie effect size 0.36), the refutation proposition holds true for any $H_0 > 70$ or $0 < \delta < 0.36$. In other words, sensitivity analysis shows that this protocol is robust and does not rely on specific constant settings.

6.2 Encoding and $\Sigma_1$ Form

Ledger $L^*$ is a finite sequence and can be uniquely encoded as a natural number $N \in \mathbb{N}$. Specifically, we adopt the Gödel numbering of finite sequences $\langle x_0, \dots, x_k \rangle$ extending the standard Cantor pairing function $\langle x, y \rangle := (x+y)(x+y+1)/2 + y$.

At this time, the refutation propositions $R_{\mathrm{C*}}$ are described using primitive recursive predicates $\theta_{\mathrm{C*}}$ as follows:

  • $R_{\mathrm{C1}} \equiv \exists i\ \theta_{\mathrm{C1}}(i)$
  • $R_{\mathrm{C4}} \equiv \exists j\ \theta_{\mathrm{C4}}(j)$
  • $R_{\mathrm{C3}} \equiv \exists k\ \theta_{\mathrm{C3}}(k)$

6.3 Implementation Scalability and Proof-Theoretic Strength

In implementation, structuring the Ledger as a Merkle Tree suppresses verification computational complexity to $O(\log N)$ and enables Partial Verification. Logically, since the Verify functions and predicates $\theta_{\mathrm{C*}}$ consist only of bounded quantification and basic arithmetic operations, this system can be fully formulated within PRA (Primitive Recursive Arithmetic) or $I\Delta_0$. This implies that ADIC operates on an extremely reliable logical foundation.

7. Conclusion and Outlook

This paper proposed the ADIC layer, which reduces the complex societal phenomenon of the "Classics Obsolescence" argument to a computable logical problem. The $\Sigma_1$-type refutation approach eliminates barren disputes and instantly filters "factual errors."

From a sociomathematical perspective, this system serves as a formal mechanism to seal the power exerted by unsubstantiated discourse on the real world using the wedge of data. As the Ledger expands, the active area of invalid claims (Unsupported region) is expected to shrink asymptotically.
